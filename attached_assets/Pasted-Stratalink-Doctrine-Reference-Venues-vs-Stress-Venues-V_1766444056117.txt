Stratalink Doctrine

Reference Venues vs Stress Venues

Version: 1.0
Status: Canonical
Applies to: TSLE, LIS, Liquidity Scoring, Consolidated Views

⸻

1. Purpose of This Doctrine

Stratalink operates on the principle that liquidity truth cannot be inferred from a single venue type.

Different exchanges exhibit structurally different liquidity behaviors:
	•	some represent clean price discovery
	•	others represent stress, leverage, and tail risk

This doctrine formally defines how Stratalink classifies venues into Reference Venues and Stress Venues, and how each is used within the TSLE (Truth-Seeking Liquidity Engine).

⸻

2. Core Definitions

2.1 Reference Venue

A Reference Venue is an exchange whose order book most closely represents clean, institutional-grade liquidity truth under normal market conditions.

Reference Venues are used to:
	•	anchor liquidity expectations
	•	calibrate TSLE thresholds
	•	define “healthy” market states

They prioritize price integrity over raw activity.

⸻

2.2 Stress Venue

A Stress Venue is an exchange whose order book reflects market extremes, including:
	•	leverage amplification
	•	liquidation cascades
	•	microstructure stress
	•	regime shifts

Stress Venues are used to:
	•	test TSLE robustness
	•	observe tail risk formation
	•	model liquidity under duress

They prioritize signal richness over cleanliness.

⸻

3. Why Stratalink Requires Both

Liquidity is state-dependent.

A venue that looks healthy in calm markets may fail catastrophically under stress, while a venue that looks chaotic may be precisely where stress is first observable.

Stratalink therefore does not choose between realism and cleanliness — it reconciles both.

Truth emerges from convergence, not consensus.

5. How TSLE Uses These Roles

5.1 Threshold Calibration
	•	Reference Venues define:
	•	acceptable spread bands
	•	expected depth curves
	•	imbalance tolerance ranges
	•	Stress Venues expand:
	•	volatility envelopes
	•	drawdown expectations
	•	stress multipliers

⸻

5.2 Regime Detection
	•	Divergence between Reference and Stress Venues is itself a signal
	•	TSLE explicitly measures:
	•	spread divergence
	•	depth decay asymmetry
	•	imbalance lead-lag behavior

This allows TSLE to identify:
	•	early stress formation
	•	liquidity illusion breakdowns
	•	regime transitions before price dislocation
